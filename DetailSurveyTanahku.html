    Redesign User Experience and User Interface of SurveyTanahku Apps.

    Freelance

    Jul 2022 - Nov 2022

    Mobile Apps

    SurveyTanahku is an application for land mapping and field surveys belonging to the ATR / BPN ministry. This application must be able to operate online and offline. Previously there was the SurveyTanahku version 1, my job was to redesign version 2 of SurveyTanahku. Of course with the new features.

    Play Demo

    Problem i want to solve.
    There are 3 main problems, 1. Old application design that doesn't refer to UI and UX principles, I have to improve the design. 2. The feature structure of the application must be rearranged to make it more concise. 3. I have to learn new terms in the field of civil engineering to be able to understand the application requirements properly.

    My Role In the Project, Who Else in the project and My responsibility.
    My role in this project is as a UI/UX Designer... actually my work is more of a UI Designer, namely updating existing application designs but I still have to add a UX "touch" to new applications. In this project i worked with 3 Android developers and 1 Project manager.

    A challenging and interesting problem for me.
    There are 2 problems with this project, first I am limited by time and money to do research. Secondly I had to learn to understand the technical terms used by the application.

    The Solutions i gave.
    In order to improve the UX of the application. To conduct research with limited time and financial resources, I decided to use a qualitative research method by way of interviews and distributing online questionnaires. This method is quite effective and efficient in collecting data from users. After doing research, I do brainstroming, combine research data and the brief given, then after getting ideas to solve existing problems I create user flows and information architecture... after that I create a wireframe that can be demonstrated, then I present results my work for the team. After my presentation I received feedback, and I revised the wireframe... after all the issues were resolved, I continued to create hi-fi wireframes (mockups). Lastly, I evaluated users regarding the application design that I had made. The evaluation was carried out by conducting Usability Testing.

    The impact of my final solution.
    unfortunately, the project that has been running for 4 months has to go into a pending period due to internal problems, so I can't see the impact of the design I made.

    I learned 2 things from this project, the first is that a UX designer must be able to decide on a research method to be carried out based on the time available and the budget provided, the method chosen must be effective and efficient. Second, to become a good designer, it never hurts to study again in order to understand context and terms from unfamiliar fields, such as in my case this time, namely civil engineering.
